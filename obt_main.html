TESTIMONIALS

Dr. Kiho Lee


Dr Lee has over 20 years of professional experience in health industry. He was born and grew up in a rural town east of Seoul. In 1992 he completed the training for Doctor of Veterinary Medicine at Seoul National University. After studying pharmacology as his speciality, he joined a biotech institute to develop a drug for arrhythmias by modulating electrical energy flow in the heart. The project was expanded through his Ph.D. thesis in which the role of the autonomic nervous system in myocardial infarction was researched. Through the doctoral degree awarded in 2005 he has entered into the world of neuroscience.

In 2006, Dr Lee moved to Dunedin to continue his research on neuroendocrinology at Otago School of Medical Sciences. His major research interest was to understand electrical circuits in the hypothalamus, a part of the brain which is in charge of homeostasis of the body. The body maintains health by keeping homeostasis through negative or positive functional feedback loops that are contributed by neuroendocrine and neuroimmune systems. If the body fails in keeping homeostasis, it causes systemic health problems such as chronic pain, chronic fatigue syndrome, diabetes, hypertension, depression, infertility and immune failure. Bowen Therapy is exceedingly effective in restoring compromised homeostasis of the body system by relieving neuromyofascial tension, which significantly underlies chronic body conditions both in humans and animals.

Mind to body approach for system healing


Mindbody therapies such as Yoga, Tai Chi and Acupuncture understands the body as an holistic energy system with much more emphasis on the interconnectiveness of each organ compared to reductionism-based approaches which view the body with a compartmentalized theoretical frame. Especially the idea of viewing the mind and the body as one being is the central concept of mind body approaches. Recent appreciation of integrative role of fascia, the tensional network in the body not only at intercellular but even at intracellular level provides more scientific rationale to the holistic healing model.

​Relieving emotional tension by rebalancing the tone of autonomic nervous system, which is caused by a shift from stress- to relaxation-mode upon Bowen moves perceived by the sensory nerve endings embedded in the myofascial tissue is a primary short-term response to the treatment. Bowen Therapy improves structural balance in the body by relieving physical tension as a secondary response to stimulating fascia. As the body has powerful endogenous regeneration mechanism exercised by the interplay between neural networks, endocrine organs, immune cells and stem cells, healing process for a wide variety of illnesses turns on once the body attains higher level of mind-body integration by releasing neuromyofascial tension. Bowen Therapy addresses the whole mind-body connection, not simply the site of injury or pain to achieve system healing that appears gradually but sustainably.
