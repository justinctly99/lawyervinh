Horses


As a type of veterinary osteopathy equine Bowen Therapy can be effective in relieving pain in the horses. The unique point of equine Bowen Therapy is that it achieves perfect body balancing between the horse and the rider by addressing both. Bowen Therapy may help the following conditions.

  • Back pain
  • Lameness
  • Soft tissue injuries
  • Sacroiliac joint subluxation
  • Temperament problem


In wild field conditions, horses need to quickly sense and react to various hazards to protect themselves from predator's life-threatening attacks. For this purpose, horses have evolved developing the sensory and musculoskeletal system which well fit for life-saving demands. However, in domesticated conditions, we live with the horses to ride them. They are expected to run and jump for people. People began to place the saddle on the horse's back to help the rider. This is the main cause of problems in the musculoskeletal system because horse's body is not designed for weight bearing in a vertical direction. Traumatic information from ill-fitting saddle or shoes, injuries from accidents and also poor riding skills is memorised not only in the nervous system but also in the tensional network--fascia, ligaments and tendons leading to unbalanced movement pattern. Pain develops in the affected area. If the horse is repeatedly ridden with painful experience, the pain memory is consolidated and eventually evolves into chronic pain causing temperament problem which gives rise to dangerous behaviour. This is to protect himself from further pain-causing experiences. Bowen Therapy achieves pain relief by manipulating connective tissue network, especially fascia as it does in humans.
